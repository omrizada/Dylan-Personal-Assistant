# Negotiation Prep

Structured negotiation preparation framework that maps your position, the counterparty's interests, and the space between. Routes to [[strategist]] for framework and strategy, then [[critic]] to challenge your assumptions about the counterparty.

## When to Use

- Before any negotiation (salary, contract, partnership, vendor, deal terms)
- When preparing for a difficult commercial conversation
- When you need to think through leverage and concessions systematically

## Arguments

- **negotiation** (required): What you are negotiating
  - Examples:
    - `"Raise discussion with board"`
    - `"Vendor contract renewal with Acme Corp"`
    - `"Partnership terms with TechCo"`
    - `"Freelancer rate negotiation for design work"`

## Instructions

1. **Gather context.** Read brain files for relevant background:
   - `brain/context/stakeholders.md` -- do we know the counterparty? What do they care about?
   - `brain/context/projects.md` -- is this negotiation tied to an active project?
   - `brain/context/decisions-log.md` -- any prior decisions or positions on this topic?
   - `brain/learnings/strategies.md` -- any negotiation patterns or lessons from past deals?

2. **Ask clarifying questions if needed.** Before building the framework, confirm:
   - What outcome does {{USER_FIRST_NAME}} want? (ideal vs. acceptable)
   - What is the relationship dynamic? (one-time vs. ongoing)
   - Are there hard constraints or non-negotiables?

3. **The [[strategist]] builds the negotiation framework:**

   ```
   NEGOTIATION PREP: [What's being negotiated]
   ============================================

   1. YOUR BATNA
   -------------
   Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement:
   - What happens if you walk away?
   - How strong is your walkaway position?
   - Walkaway point: [specific threshold]

   2. COUNTERPARTY INTERESTS
   -------------------------
   What they really want (not just their stated position):
   - Interest 1: [underlying need]
   - Interest 2: [underlying need]
   - Likely constraints they face: [budget, timeline, politics]
   - What success looks like for them: [outcome]

   3. ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement)
   ------------------------------------
   Where your interests overlap:
   - Your range: [minimum acceptable] to [ideal outcome]
   - Their likely range: [estimated minimum] to [estimated ideal]
   - Overlap area: [where a deal lives]
   - If no overlap: [creative options to expand the pie]

   4. LEVERAGE POINTS
   ------------------
   Cards you hold:
   - [Leverage 1] — [why it matters to them]
   - [Leverage 2] — [why it matters to them]
   Cards they hold:
   - [Counter-leverage 1] — [how to neutralize]

   5. CONCESSION STRATEGY
   ----------------------
   What you can give up cheaply that they value highly:
   - [Concession 1]: Low cost to you, high value to them
   - [Concession 2]: Low cost to you, high value to them
   What to never concede:
   - [Non-negotiable 1] — [why]

   6. OPENING POSITION
   -------------------
   Where to start: [specific opening]
   Why: [anchoring rationale]
   First move: [who should make it and why]

   7. ROLE-PLAY: COUNTERPARTY RESPONSES
   ------------------------------------
   If you open with [X], they will likely:
   - Response scenario A: [response] → Your counter: [move]
   - Response scenario B: [response] → Your counter: [move]
   - Response scenario C: [hardball] → Your counter: [move]
   ```

4. **The [[critic]] challenges assumptions.** Specifically:
   - Are you overestimating your leverage?
   - Are you underestimating theirs?
   - What are you assuming about their interests that might be wrong?
   - What information are you missing?
   - What is the worst-case response to your opening?

5. **Output directly in conversation.** Do not save to a file unless {{USER_FIRST_NAME}} asks.

6. **Offer follow-up options:**
   - "Want to role-play the full negotiation? I'll play [counterparty]."
   - "Want me to draft the opening email/message?"
   - "Want me to research comparable deals for benchmarking?" (triggers `/research`)
